## Quadratic Equations
The solutions to a quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ are given by the formula:
$$ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$
- If $b^2 - 4ac > 0$, there are two real roots.
- If $b^2 - 4ac = 0$, there is one real root.
- If $b^2 - 4ac < 0$, there are two complex roots.

## Progressions
**Arithmetic Progression (AP):**
$n$-th term: $a_n = a + (n-1)d$
Sum of first $n$ terms: $S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a + (n-1)d]$

**Geometric Progression (GP):**
$n$-th term: $a_n = a r^{n-1}$
Sum of first $n$ terms (if $r \neq 1$): $S_n = a \frac{1 - r^n}{1 - r}$
Sum of infinite GP (if $|r| < 1$): $S_\infty = \frac{a}{1 - r}$

## Binomial Theorem
$$(x + y)^n = \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} x^{n-k} y^k$$
